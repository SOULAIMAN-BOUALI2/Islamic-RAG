When the difference grew wider, the Messenger of Allâh [pbuh] suspended any solution whereof until the Revelation was sent down. ‘Ubadah bin As-Samit said: "We went out with the Messenger of Allâh [pbuh] and I witnessed Badr with him. The battle started and Allâh, the Exalted, defeated the enemy. Some of the Muslims sought and pursued the enemy, some were intent on collecting the spoils from the enemy camp, and others were guarding the Messenger of Allâh [pbuh] and were on the alert for any emergency or surprise attack. When night came and the Muslims gathered together, those who had collected the booty said: "We collected it, so no one else has any right to it." Those who had pursued the enemy said: "You do not have more right to it than we do; we held the enemy at bay and then defeated them." As for the men who had been guarding the Prophet [pbuh], they also made similar claims to the spoils. At that very time, a Qur’ânic verse was revealed saying: "They ask you (O Muhammad [pbuh]) about the spoils of war. Say: ‘The spoils are for Allâh and the Messenger.’
