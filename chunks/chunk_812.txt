From that time he started calling at the Prophet [pbuh] in the mornings and in the evenings. [Za'd Al-Ma'ad 2/205] On the authority of Ibn Ishaq, when the Prophet [pbuh] made him sit down in front of him in his house, the Prophet said, "O ‘Adi, were you not cast in disbelief?" "Yes". ‘Adi said. "Did you not share one quarter of your people’s gains?" "Yes". Said ‘Adi. The Messenger of Allâh [pbuh] said: "It is sinful in your religion to do such a thing, and you should not allow yourself to do it." "Yes, by Allâh, that is true", said ‘Adi. "Thus I worked out that he was a Prophet inspired by Allâh, and sent to people. He knows what is unknown." [Ibn Hisham 2/581] In another version, the Prophet [pbuh] said: "‘Adi, embrace Islam and you shall be secure." "But I am a man of religion." Said ‘Adi. "I know your religion better than you."
